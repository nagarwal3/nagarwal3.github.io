Masala Chai

Everyone should have at least one good party trick (so I am told)....I am not particularly good at telling jokes, nor am I an ace in the kitchen, but I do know how to make a quick and simiple cup of Masala Chai. The key word here is simple—this is not grandma's recipe. This is a recipe for those people who are short on time, attention span, and like having friends over on the fly (because they are never quite planned out enough to throw a proper dinner party).

Directions

Adjustments

sweet•ness

Feel free to add as much or as little sugar as you want! You can even skip adding the sugar initially to the pot and let each person add sugar to taste per cup...1 lump or 2 or 3...?!?!

spicy•ness

For a little more bite to your chai add more ginger and cardamom. Grating fresh ginger especially adds a kick. Some people add fennel seed or star anise for a gentle licorice note.

strength

If you like your chai to be stronger skimp on the milk or leave the tea bags in the pot— they will continue to brew and add strength! If you prefer a milder chai, take the teabags out sooner or even use a decaffinated black tea.
